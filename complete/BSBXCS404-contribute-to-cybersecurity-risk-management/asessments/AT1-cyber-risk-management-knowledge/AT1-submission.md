### Question 2

**There are 13 Australian Privacy Principles. Which of the following is NOT an Australian Privacy Principle? There may be more than one answer.**
- [x] Personal information ownership - Individuals retain the ownership of their own personal information.    
- [ ] Quality of personal information - reasonable steps are taken to ensure personal information collected is accurate and up to date and complete.
- [ ] Notification of the collection of personal information - circumstances in which an entity that collects personal information must tell an individual about certain matters.
- [ ] Direct marketing - use of personal information for direct marketing purposes if certain conditions are met.
- [ ] Anonymity and pseudonymity - Individuals have option of not identifying themselves in collected personal information.
- [x] Prohibition on selling Personal Information - Personal Information must not be sold for profit to any other organization or group.

There are 13 privacy principles. You correctly identified two principles that are NOT part of the Privacy Act principles.
