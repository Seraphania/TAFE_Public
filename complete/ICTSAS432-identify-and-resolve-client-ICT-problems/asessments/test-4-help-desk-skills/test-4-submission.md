### Question 1
Unmarked 	

**In your own words, What is [customer service etiquette?](https://www.revechat.com/blog/customer-service-etiquette/)**  

Selected Answer: 	
```
Chosen Guideline: "Give customers your full attention & take good notes "

Let's say a client communicated that they were having intermittent internet issues; I would focus on what exact issues they were experiencing, and by paying attention might identify areas where I need more information in determining the problem, so I could ask follow up questions. I would take notes as they were describing the issue, and confirm with the client that I have correctly noted important information, like how they are affected, what their needs are, as well as things like when and how often the issue is occuring.

There are two reasons for this; it gives me more information that may be useful to solve the problem, and it also helps the client to feel heard and supported, meaming that they are likely to trust me to help them again in future.
```

### Question 2
Unmarked 	

**Which [three support skills](https://support.cc/what-skills-are-required-to-work-with-help-desk-system) do you think are the most important? Why?**  

Selected Answer: 	
```
Being Empathetic, Paying Attention to detail and Having Business Awareness. While all of the support skills are important, I beleive that having a good balance of these three skills will set an IT Support worker up to be able to practice the other skills as well.

Satisfying customers is important, but that is not possible if you are not able to empathise with them, understand the problem by paying close attention to the customer, their report and other information, and having awareness of their business needs and your own.

I think all the other skills flow from these three core skills in a similar way.
```

### Question 4
Unmarked 	

**In your own words, summarize the [different priority levels](https://www.jitbit.com/news/helpdesk-ticket-priority-levels/).**  

Selected Answer: 	
```
Priority 1: The "Drop everything and fix this now" type issues. A client cannot do business without it being fixed, so it needs to be resolved ASAP. As well as this, communication needs to be provided early and often.

Priority 2: Issues that need fixing so that a client or business can work most efficiently. These are less time-sensitive, but still important. What I woudl mostly be doing between emergencies.

Proirity 3: "Backburner issues", these should certainly be tracked, and time should also be allocated to these issues, but they certainly fall behind the other two.
```
