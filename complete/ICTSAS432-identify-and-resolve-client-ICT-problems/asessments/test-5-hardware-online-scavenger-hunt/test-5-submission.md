### Question 5
Unmarked 	

**Why should a person buy a SSD instead of a HDD? Give reasons for your answer.**  
[Link](https://au.pcmag.com/ssd-1/8831/ssd-vs-hdd-whats-the-difference)


Selected Answer: 	
```
Because if the flash chips contained in an SSD, data is able to be accessed much more quickly than on traditional "spinning platter" HDD's, because the read head does not head does not have to seek a position on the hard drive platter.
For this reason Solid State Drives (SSD's) are preferred over Hard Disk Drives for settings where data needs to be accessed quickly. They are most commonly used for the main Root drive of a computer (for example C drive in windows) because data is frequently being read/written there during the running of an OS.

because of the cost of production however, SSD's are more expensive to produce (and therefor buy) and storage capacity is more expensive per GB than for SSD's. A good compromise is typically to store program files (Operating system, Program install data, game install data etc) on and SSD and if more space is needed for storage of things like media files, which don't require such high speeds for access, then they are stored on a larger, cheaper HDD, where the loss of access speed is less noticible during normal operation.

Because of the lack of moving parts, SSD's are are somewhat more durable than HDD's and will better tolerate movement (such as in a laptop), however neither are preferred for long term archival storage: for long term archival, where large amounts of data must be retained for long periods, without needing to be accessed frquently tape is prefferd due to it's low cost and ability to be stored for long periods. Source: I read 9-track tapes written in the 60's -90's frequently in my job.
```
